Key Facts

ㅇ as placeholder: When a syllable starts with a vowel sound, ㅇ is written silently: 아 = a, 이 = i, 오 = o.
Reading order: Left→right for vertical vowels (가, 나), top→bottom for horizontal vowels (모, 보), then final consonant at the bottom.
ㄹ changes by position: As initial → sounds like r. As final (받침) → sounds like l. Example: 라 = ra, 말 = mal.
받침 (batchim) = the final consonant at the bottom of a syllable. There are 5 pronunciation rules that govern how consonants change when they meet across syllable boundaries. Master these and you'll sound natural fast.

All batchim reduce to one of just 7 sounds when the syllable stands alone or precedes another consonant.

Rule 1 — Linking 연음화

Rule 1 Batchim "slides" to the next syllable
When a batchim syllable is followed by ㅇ (silent placeholder), the batchim moves over to become the initial of the next syllable.
받침 + ㅇ__ → (no batchim) + (받침)__
Trick: ㅇ is a vacuum — it sucks the consonant from the syllable before it.
먹어→[머거] meo-geoeat (informal)
읽어→[일거] il-georead (informal)
있어→[이써] i-sseohave / exist
한국어→[한구거] han-gu-geoKorean language
옷이→[오시] o-siclothes (subject)
ㅎ exception: ㅎ as batchim before ㅇ is dropped entirely — not linked.
좋아요→[조아요] jo-a-yogood / I like it
많아요→[마나요] ma-na-yothere is a lot

Rule 2 — Nasalization 비음화

Rule 2 Hard stops soften before nasal sounds
When batchim ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ precedes ㄴ or ㅁ, it changes to its matching nasal.
ㄱ→ㅇ  |  ㄷ→ㄴ  |  ㅂ→ㅁ    (before ㄴ or ㅁ)
Trick: Nasality is contagious — ㄱ→ㅇ (throat), ㄷ→ㄴ (tongue), ㅂ→ㅁ (lips).

ㄱ → ㅇ

국민→[궁민] gung-mincitizen
작년→[장년] jang-nyeonlast year
먹는다→[멍는다] meong-neun-dais eating

ㄷ → ㄴ

닫는다→[단는다] dan-neun-dais closing
있는→[인는] in-neunthat exists
믿는다→[민는다] min-neun-dabelieves

ㅂ → ㅁ

입문→[임문] im-munintroduction
없는→[엄는] eom-neunthat doesn't exist
밥맛→[밤맛] bam-mattaste of rice

Rule 3 — Lateralization 유음화

Rule 3 ㄴ becomes ㄹ next to ㄹ
When ㄴ and ㄹ meet across a syllable boundary (either order), both become ㄹ.
ㄹ+ㄴ → ㄹ+ㄹ    ㄴ+ㄹ → ㄹ+ㄹ
Trick: ㄹ is contagious — it turns any neighboring ㄴ into itself.
신라→[실라] sil-laSilla dynasty
연락→[열락] yeol-lakcontact
칼날→[칼랄] kal-lalknife blade
설날→[설랄] seol-lalLunar New Year

Rule 4 — Aspiration 격음화

Rule 4 ㅎ merges with plosives to create aspirated consonants
ㅎ (breath consonant) fuses with ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅈ in either order, producing their aspirated forms.
ㅎ+ㄱ→ㅋ  |  ㅎ+ㄷ→ㅌ  |  ㅎ+ㅂ→ㅍ  |  ㅎ+ㅈ→ㅊ
Trick: ㅎ adds a puff of air to whatever consonant it touches.

ㅎ as batchim

좋다→[조타] jo-tato be good
많다→[만타] man-tamany / a lot
좋지→[조치] jo-chiright? isn't it good?
넣고→[네코] ne-koput in (and…)

ㅎ as initial (after batchim)

먹히다→[머키다] meo-ki-dato be eaten (passive)
잡히다→[자피다] ja-pi-dato be caught (passive)
읽히다→[일키다] il-ki-dato be read (passive)

Rule 5 — Tensification 경음화

Rule 5 Consonants tighten after a hard stop batchim
After a batchim that sounds like ㄱ, ㄷ, or ㅂ, the following ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅅ/ㅈ becomes tense (doubled).
after [ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ]:   ㄱ→ㄲ, ㄷ→ㄸ, ㅂ→ㅃ, ㅅ→ㅆ, ㅈ→ㅉ
Trick: A hard stop tensions the throat — the next consonant comes out tense too.
학교→[학꾜] hak-kkyoschool
식당→[식땅] sik-ttangrestaurant
없다→[업따] eop-ttato not have/exist
닦다→[닥따] dak-ttato wipe / clean
입구→[입꾸] ip-kkuentrance
박수→[박쑤] bak-ssuapplause

Bonus Two final consonants — only one is pronounced

Rules Priority

When multiple rules could apply: Aspiration (ㅎ) → Nasalization → Lateralization → Linking → Tensification. In practice you rarely stack more than two.
